# FixTrack — Two-Minute Pitch Talking Points

## Problem

- Residents submit a request, then hear nothing
- No status, no timeline, no confirmation work was done
- Students resubmit tickets, call housing, ask RAs out of frustration
- FMS receives vague, incomplete requests — technicians show up without the right context
- Both sides lose time to poor communication

## Insight

- Package delivery already solved this — FedEx/UPS show stages, an expected date, and a delivery photo
- Maintenance requests follow the same lifecycle, just without the communication layer

## Solution

- Integrates into CMU's existing maintenance web app
- **Stage tracking** — Submitted, Under Review, Scheduled, In Progress, Completed + estimated resolution date
- **Photo confirmation** — technician photos the finished work as proof of completion
- **Notifications** — residents notified at every stage change automatically

## Why It Works

- Residents get visibility and trust
- FMS gets structured info, fewer duplicates, better-prepared technicians
- Layers onto the existing system — no rip-and-replace

## Next Step

- Building a working prototype of the tracking flow, notifications, and photo confirmation

## Q&A Prep

- Add-on layer, not a new platform
- Photo step takes seconds on a phone
- Proven pattern at scale (FedEx, Amazon, DoorDash)
- Photos capture the work, not the resident's space
- Every feature tied to customer interview findings
